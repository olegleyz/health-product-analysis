# 01 — Vision Document

**Agent:** Vision Agent (Principal Product Manager)

---

## Problem

People already know what healthy behavior looks like. The barrier is not knowledge — it is **consistency and attention**.

Life constantly pulls focus away from health decisions. Small deviations — fewer steps, worse sleep, heavier meals, skipped workouts — accumulate silently until they become real problems.

Every health tool available today is **passive**. Apps wait to be opened. Dashboards wait to be checked. Even AI assistants wait for questions. Nothing actively watches over your routine and intervenes when it matters.

There is no system that behaves like someone who **genuinely cares** about your health and checks in first.

---

## Core User

**Primary persona:** A health-aware adult (30–55) who:

- Understands what healthy behavior looks like
- Has goals around fitness, nutrition, sleep, and habits
- Struggles with consistency due to a busy life (work, family, travel, stress)
- Does not want to spend time managing dashboards or tracking apps
- Wants to feel like someone is watching out for them
- May or may not use wearables or health apps

**Key insight:** The user does not need more information. They need someone paying attention.

---

## Unique Value

The concierge is the **first health system that engages you, rather than waiting for you to engage it**.

| Existing tools | Health Concierge |
|---|---|
| Passive — waits for you to open the app | **Proactive — reaches out first** |
| Data-centric — shows charts and numbers | **Behavior-centric — focuses on decisions** |
| Requires discipline to use consistently | **Creates accountability by checking in** |
| One-size-fits-all notifications | **Personalized, context-aware nudges** |
| Sensor-dependent | **Works with or without sensors** |

The unique value is **continuous, caring attention** — not data, not dashboards, not information.

---

## Core Concierge Behaviors

1. **Morning check-in** — Asks about the day's plan: workout intentions, meals, energy level
2. **Proactive nudges** — Sends timely reminders based on context (pre-workout nutrition, bedtime, hydration)
3. **Follow-up** — Asks how things went: "Did you train today?" "How was dinner?"
4. **Drift detection** — Notices when patterns change: activity drops, sleep shifts, nutrition deteriorates
5. **Gentle accountability** — Asks caring questions rather than issuing commands: "Your activity dropped this week. Everything okay?"
6. **Contextual guidance** — Provides actionable, specific advice at the moment it matters
7. **Adaptive learning** — Gets better over time by learning habits, preferences, goals, and challenges

---

## Success Criteria

1. User engages with the concierge at least once daily (response rate > 60% on check-ins)
2. User reports feeling "someone is watching out for me" (qualitative)
3. Measurable improvement in at least one health metric within 30 days (sleep consistency, workout frequency, or nutrition quality)
4. User does not feel nagged — engagement feels caring, not annoying (< 10% opt-out from nudges)
5. System operates effectively without any sensor data — pure conversation is sufficient

---

## Non-Goals / Constraints

### Non-goals
- **Not a medical advisor** — Does not diagnose, prescribe, or replace professional medical guidance
- **Not a fitness app** — Does not create detailed workout programs or meal plans from scratch
- **Not a data analytics platform** — Does not aim to build comprehensive health dashboards
- **Not a social platform** — This is a private, 1:1 relationship between user and concierge

### Constraints
- Must work via simple messaging (WhatsApp or similar) — no app install required for basic use
- Must never feel like spam — frequency and tone must be carefully managed
- Must handle silence gracefully — if the user doesn't respond, back off intelligently
- Must be safe — never give dangerous health advice, always defer to professionals for medical concerns
- Privacy-first — user health data must be treated with extreme care
